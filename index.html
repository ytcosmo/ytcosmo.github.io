About me

Dr. Yuting Wang is a researcher of cosmology. Her research interests are large-scale structure and cosmology. Her primary work involves studying the statistical properties of galaxy distribution, such as the two-point correlation function, redshift-space distortions, and baryon acoustic oscillations, to unravel the fundamental physics driving the expansion of the universe.

She has contributed to several major projects in the field, including BOSS (Baryon Oscillation Spectroscopic Survey), eBOSS (extended BOSS), DESI (Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument), and PFS (Prime Focus Spectrograph). These projects aim to map the large-scale structure of the universe, providing critical data to improve our understanding of dark energy and the evolution of the universe.
